About

Rachel Wuese Tyona

Rachel is a visionary accomplished leader with a background in Medical Physiology. Known for her resilience and innovation, she has extensive experience in community engagement. She made history as the First Female Speaker of the Benue State Youth Parliament. Her advocacy for youth-centric policies has earned her the moniker 'the speaking speaker.' As Executive Director of Ngodoo Development Initiative, Rachel leads projects empowering youth and women. With over 10 years of experience designing and implementing strategies, She has successfully led and participated in numerous projects across public, private, and business sectors. She holds a Certificate in Project Management from the University of Washington, further enhancing her leadership

From

The desk of the Executive Director

Fun Fact: In Tiv language, ‘Ngodoo’ means ‘mother is good’. A good mother will always ensure the total well-being of her child(ren) no matter the circumstances.

A warm welcome to our digital home! As the Executive Director of Ngodoo Development Initiative, I feel honored to lead an organization dedicated to ensuring the growth and development of the society, creating sustainable environment and positively transforming the total lives of youth and women. Through our website, you get to explore and see first hand the impactful work we do, the communities we serve and the transformative projects we undertake. Here, you will find comprehensive information about our programs, initiatives, and how you can get involved. I invite you to navigate through our pages, learn about our mission, and discover the thousands ways you can join us in making a difference. Together, we can create positive change and build a brighter future for all. Thank you for visiting, and we look forward to connecting with you on this journey of impact and empowerment.

Warm Regards
